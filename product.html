Categories of Clothing

  • Tops — Includes shirts, blouses, polos, t-shirts, tank tops, flannels, sweaters, and hoodies.
  • Bottoms — Pants, jeans, skirts, shorts, joggers, and leggings.
  • Dresses — Casual day dresses, formal wear, gowns, sundresses, and vintage statement pieces.
  • Outerwear — Jackets, coats, blazers, trench coats, windbreakers, and cardigans.
  • Clothing Accessories — Scarves, hats, belts, gloves, ties, bags, wallets, and sometimes shoes.

Condition Levels

  • New — Surplus Items may still have tags or look unworn. Fresh fabric with no fading or wear; near store-quality.
  • Good — Light signs of use (minor fading or pilling) but no major flaws like tears or stains; everyday wearable.
  • Fair — Noticeable wear (fading, stretched seams, small repairs). Functional and often chosen by upcyclers or for a worn-in look.
  • Poor — Heavy wear with visible flaws (stains, holes, thinning fabric, frays). Best for DIY, repurposing, or distressed/vintage style.
